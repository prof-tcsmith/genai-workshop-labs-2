# Coverage Policy CP-201 — Advanced Imaging: Lumbar Spine MRI

**SYNTHETIC teaching material. Not a real medical policy. Not medical advice.**

## Scope
Prior authorization for outpatient MRI of the lumbar spine (CPT 72148) for a member
presenting with low back pain.

## Medical-necessity criteria
A request is **approved** when ALL of the following are documented:

1. **Conservative therapy trial.** At least **6 weeks** of conservative management
   (physical therapy, NSAIDs, or activity modification) has been completed and
   documented, OR a red-flag criterion (below) is present.
2. **Persistent or progressive symptoms** after that trial, OR objective neurologic
   findings (e.g., radiculopathy, motor weakness, reflex changes).
3. **No recent equivalent imaging.** No lumbar MRI in the prior **12 months** unless
   there is a documented significant change in clinical status.

## Red flags (expedite — conservative-therapy trial NOT required)
- Suspected cauda equina syndrome (new bowel/bladder dysfunction, saddle anesthesia).
- Suspected malignancy, infection, or fracture (e.g., history of cancer,
  unexplained weight loss, fever, significant trauma, IV drug use).
- Progressive neurologic deficit.

## Deny when
- Conservative therapy has not been tried AND no red flag is present.
- Duplicate imaging within 12 months without a documented change in status.

## Pend for more information when
- The conservative-therapy duration is not documented.
- Neurologic exam findings are not documented and no red flag is stated.
