# Coverage Policy CP-330 — Specialty Drug: Biologic for Moderate–Severe Plaque Psoriasis

**SYNTHETIC teaching material. Not a real medical policy. Not medical advice.**
The drug name below ("Dermolimab") is fictional.

## Scope
Prior authorization for the specialty biologic **Dermolimab** (self-injectable) for
moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis.

## Step-therapy and medical-necessity criteria
A request is **approved** when ALL of the following are documented:

1. **Diagnosis.** Moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis confirmed by a dermatologist,
   with body-surface-area involvement **≥ 10%** OR involvement of a high-impact site
   (face, palms, soles, or genitals).
2. **Step therapy — topicals.** Trial and inadequate response to (or documented
   intolerance/contraindication of) at least one **topical** therapy (e.g., a topical
   corticosteroid or vitamin-D analog) for **≥ 4 weeks**.
3. **Step therapy — conventional systemic OR phototherapy.** Trial and inadequate
   response to (or intolerance/contraindication of) at least one **conventional
   systemic agent** (e.g., methotrexate) **or** a course of **phototherapy**.
4. **Safety screening.** A negative or appropriately treated **tuberculosis (TB)
   screen** is documented before starting the biologic.
5. **Prescriber.** Prescribed by, or in consultation with, a dermatologist.

## Deny when
- Step therapy (criteria 2 and 3) has not been tried and no intolerance or
  contraindication is documented.

## Pend for more information when
- The TB screening result is not documented.
- Body-surface-area involvement or the affected site is not documented.

## Approval duration
Initial approval is for **6 months**; continuation requires documented response.
